About Me

Kitten is actually called Soph, but Soph Codes didn't have the same effect. Anyway, I know some stuff about writing code.

I started twiddling with code around seven years ago while working at the NHS. I had some free time, so wrote a VB.NET program that was a front-end to their asset database. Shortly after, I spent some time working with my Dad on a concept website that I had been thinking about. Over several months, I picked up a good grounding in HTML and CSS, and was learning ASP.NET, ASP.NET Membership, SQL and Ajax implementation. I also developed the brand for the website, creating beautiful graphics for both website and stationery.
View Obsession Girls Project.

This spawned my love of code and design, and I have been developing these skills (when I’ve had the time!) ever since.

In 2012, I started a raw chocolate business (alongside my IT job!). As well as creating recipes, I was the sole developer behind the website and sole designer of the brand. I learned E-Commerce so that I could sell my products online and gained extensive knowledge of packaging design. I sold the company in 2015 as I wanted to focus back on IT – I’d made enough chocolate by then!

As well as all this shiny code stuff, I also have (almost 10 years!) experience of Windows & Linux server administration, LDAP & Active Directory, user support and general Systems Administration type things! I have been working as an IT Manager for the last six years, and had been working towards several Microsoft certifications (MTA, MCSA, and MCSE). After achieving my MTA, I realised my passion really does lie with coding. I finished up a few projects I was working on, and jumped head first into a Full-Stack developer course. I’ve increased my knowledge of HTML and CSS, learned about Bootstrap, and am now learning JavaScript. I will be moving onto PHP and Ruby/Rails in the near future.

As you may have guessed, I have a serious ambition to learn. I absolutely love learning new things and applying my newly learned skills to projects. Handily, I tend to pick things up fairly quickly too.
